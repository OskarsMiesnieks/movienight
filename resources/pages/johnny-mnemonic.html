Johnny Mnemonic

1h 36m

Plot Summary:

"Johnny Mnemonic" is a cyberpunk science fiction film directed by Robert Longo and released in 1995. Loosely based on a short story by William Gibson, a pioneer of the cyberpunk genre, the film is set in a dystopian future where the world is connected by a vast computer network, and individuals can augment their brains with cybernetic implants to store information.

The story revolves around Johnny Mnemonic, portrayed by Keanu Reeves, a data courier who transports sensitive information by storing it in his brain implant. In a world where corporate interests and organized crime intersect, Johnny becomes a target when he is entrusted with a massive data package containing valuable and dangerous information.

As he attempts to deliver the data before it kills him due to its size, Johnny finds himself pursued by various adversaries, including Yakuza assassins, a crazed preacher, and a powerful corporation.

During his journey, Johnny teams up with Jane, a bodyguard played by Dina Meyer, and a cybernetically enhanced dolphin named Jones. Together, they navigate the dangerous landscape of a high-tech future, dealing with virtual reality, hackers, and the ethical implications of technology.
