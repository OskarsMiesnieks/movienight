Jurassic World: Fallen Kingdom

2018
2h 8m

Stars: Chris Pratt, Bryce Dallas Howard, Rafe Spall

Synopsis

"Jurassic World: Fallen Kingdom" is the fifth installment in the "Jurassic Park" film series, directed by J.A. Bayona and released in 2018. The movie is a direct sequel to "Jurassic World" (2015) and continues the story set on Isla Nublar.

Following the events of "Jurassic World," the island of Isla Nublar is threatened by an active volcanic eruption. The remaining dinosaurs face extinction once again as the island's dormant volcano is about to erupt.

Claire Dearing (played by Bryce Dallas Howard), the former park operations manager, has now founded the Dinosaur Protection Group, an organization advocating for the rights and protection of dinosaurs. With the eruption imminent, Claire is approached by Eli Mills (played by Rafe Spall), a representative of Benjamin Lockwood (played by James Cromwell), the former partner of Jurassic Park's founder, John Hammond.

Lockwood, now in ill health, reveals his plan to rescue the dinosaurs from the island and transport them to a sanctuary where they can live safely away from human interference. Claire agrees to help and recruits Owen Grady (played by Chris Pratt), the Velociraptor trainer, to assist in the mission.

Once on the island, Claire, Owen, and a team of scientists and mercenaries begin rescuing the dinosaurs. However, they soon discover that Mills has a different agenda. Instead of saving the dinosaurs, Mills plans to sell them to the highest bidders, including military organizations and private collectors, to profit from their genetic material.

As the team races against time to save the dinosaurs from exploitation and the impending volcanic eruption, they face numerous dangers, including new genetically-engineered dinosaurs and the moral dilemma of whether these creatures should be saved or left to perish.

The story takes a darker turn as the film explores themes of greed, exploitation, and the consequences of playing with nature. "Jurassic World: Fallen Kingdom" combines thrilling action sequences with ethical questions about the rights and preservation of genetically-engineered creatures, making it a visually stunning and thought-provoking addition to the "Jurassic Park" series.
